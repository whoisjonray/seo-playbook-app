Full Strategy Details

Gradual Increase in Content Posting Velocity

What It Is: Slowly ramping up the frequency of posting content or threads to avoid triggering spam filters on platforms or in search engines.
Why It Works: Sudden spikes in posting volume are seen as spammy behavior by algorithms, which can suppress visibility or penalize accounts; a gradual increase mimics organic growth, helping maintain trust and visibility.
When To Use: When launching new content repurposing strategies on social platforms or when scaling content production efforts on new or existing domains.
Execution Steps:

  • Start posting at a low frequency, e.g., one thread every five days.
  • Increase to one every three days, then daily, and eventually two times per day if sustainable.
  • Monitor account health and traffic metrics throughout.
  • Adjust pacing based on platform response and engagement rates.
    Pitfalls & Limits:
  • Posting too slowly may limit potential growth speed.
  • Jumping too quickly can trigger spam filters.
  • Platform tolerance varies; what works for one site might differ for another; constant monitoring is key.
    Example: The recommendation for threads and X is to avoid going from zero to high posting velocity immediately to evade spam filters.
