Hierarchical Website Structure & Hub Pages

Hierarchical Website Structure & Hub Pages

Category: Content Strategy & Keywords

Full Strategy Details

  1. Hierarchical Website Structure & Hub Pages
    What It Is: Organize your website into a clear hierarchy with parent pages and child pages that cover topics in greater depth, and create hub pages linking to bottom-of-funnel SEO landing pages, often placed in the footer or navigation.
    Why It Works: Google favors well-structured sites with clear information architecture as it signals topical relevance and credibility. Hub pages provide a natural, keyword-focused pathway for users and search engines, improving crawl efficiency and internal link equity distribution.
    When To Use: Effective for most websites, especially ecommerce, service providers, and content-heavy sites aiming to target bottom-of-funnel, purchase-intent keywords. Also valuable for both small and enterprise sites needing strong site architecture.
    Execution Steps:
    1. Identify primary topics and create parent (hub) pages named with keyword-aligned terms like “Services,” “Products,” or “Collections.”
    2. Develop child pages under these hubs that cover subtopics or related keywords in-depth.
    3. Place the hub pages in the footer or main navigation menu naturally to increase visibility.
    4. Optimize page titles, URLs, meta descriptions, H1s, and opening sentences with targeted keywords.
    5. Link child pages back to the hub page and between related child pages to reinforce structure.
      Pitfalls & Limits:
    • Forcing too many links in navigation can clutter user experience.
    • Poorly optimized or irrelevant hub pages can confuse users and dilute keyword focus.
    • Overly flat or deep structures without clear hubs reduce crawl efficiency.
      Example: Using a “Services” hub page in the footer to group various purchase intent landing pages with optimized titles and URLs, providing authentic, keyword-focused pathways that both users and Google prefer.
