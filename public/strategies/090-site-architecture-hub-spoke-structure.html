Full Strategy Details

Site Architecture Hub & Spoke Structure

What It Is: Organize your website into clear hub pages (category or topic hubs) that house related SEO landing pages and resources, ensuring important pages remain within two clicks from the homepage.
Why It Works: Pages closer to the homepage receive higher crawl priority and link equity from Google, improving their ranking potential; a logical structure also enhances user experience and helps search engines understand site hierarchy.
When To Use: Medium to large websites with many landing pages, ecommerce sites, local businesses with multiple location pages, and content-heavy sites wanting better SEO page prioritization.
Execution Steps:

  1. Identify key categories or themes (e.g., alternatives, locations, services).
  2. Create hub pages (e.g., yoursite.com/alternatives/) that act as parent pages.
  3. Place all related subpages (e.g., competitor alternatives) under these hubs with URL structures like yoursite.com/alternatives/competitor-name.
  4. Link hub pages prominently in site footer and/or navigation so they’re accessible within two clicks from homepage.
  5. Avoid burying important SEO pages too deep in pagination or nested menus.
    Pitfalls & Limits: Over-nesting beyond two clicks reduces page priority; cluttered or unnatural URL structures confuse users and bots; forcing hub pages without thematic relevance dilutes SEO value.
    Example: Bitrix24’s extensive alternatives pages (e.g., bitrix24.com/alternatives/freehive-alternative) rank well because they are housed under a clear /alternatives/ hub within two clicks from homepage.
