Full Strategy Details

  1. Submit Sitemap.xml to Google as a Best Practice but Not Mandatory
    What It Is: Providing a sitemap.xml to Google Search Console assists crawl and indexation but is not strictly required for Google to find and index pages.
    Why It Works: A sitemap guides Google bots explicitly to site URLs, especially important for large or deeply nested sites, improving crawl efficiency.
    When To Use: Large websites, new sites with many pages, or sites with pages hard to discover by crawling alone; less critical if site is viral or has many backlinks.
    Execution Steps:
    • Create a sitemap.xml listing all important URLs.
    • Upload the sitemap.xml to your website root folder.
    • Submit the sitemap URL in Google Search Console under “Sitemaps.”
    • Regularly update and resubmit sitemap after major site changes.
      Pitfalls & Limits: Sitemap submission is not a fix for poor site architecture; does not guarantee ranking improvements alone; small or well-linked sites may not benefit much.
      Example: The podcast explained that Google crawls sites anyway but sitemap speeds up discovery, especially for buried or high-authority pages.
