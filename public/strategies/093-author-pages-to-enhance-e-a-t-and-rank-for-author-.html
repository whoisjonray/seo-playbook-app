Author Pages to Enhance E-A-T and Rank for Author Names

Category: Content Strategy & Keywords

Metadata

  • Type: Content Strategy & Keywords
  • Difficulty: Intermediate
  • Time to Impact: 1-3 Months
  • Required Tools: None specified

Quick Summary

Create dedicated author directory pages listing an author’s content and providing detailed author bios to capture searches for author names and boost expertise signals.

Full Strategy Details

  1. Author Pages to Enhance E-A-T and Rank for Author Names
    What It Is: Create dedicated author directory pages listing an author’s content and providing detailed author bios to capture searches for author names and boost expertise signals.
    Why It Works: Builds author credibility, aligns with Google’s E-A-T focus by emphasizing expertise and trustworthiness, and improves chances for author name-related traffic.
    When To Use: Content-heavy sites, blogs, news publishers, sites with multiple authors contributing regularly.
    Execution Steps:
    1. For each author, make a dedicated author page with a well-written bio describing their credentials, area of expertise, and contributions.
    2. List all articles or content pieces by the author on that page.
    3. Internally link from articles to author pages and vice versa.
    4. Optimize author pages for author name keywords.
      Pitfalls & Limits: Thin or poorly written bios miss opportunity to build trust; if authors are not known or authoritative, may not add much SEO value; duplicative content across author pages can dilute signals.
      Example: The speaker uses edward.com as example site with author directories to rank for author names.
