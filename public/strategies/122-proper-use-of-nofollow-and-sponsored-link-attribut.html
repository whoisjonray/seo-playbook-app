Full Strategy Details

  1. Proper Use of NoFollow and Sponsored Link Attributes
    What It Is: Mark paid, affiliate, or untrusted outbound links with ‚Äúnofollow‚Äù or ‚Äúsponsored‚Äù link attributes to prevent passing SEO authority (link juice).
    Why It Works: Google discourages passing link equity through paid or manipulative links; marking them prevents penalties and maintains site trust.
    When To Use: Affiliate links, paid advertisements, sponsored content, and untrusted external links.
    Execution Steps:
    1. Identify all outbound affiliate or paid links on your site.
    2. Add rel="nofollow" or preferably rel="sponsored" attributes to those links.
    3. Double-check implementation using tools like MozBar to verify link attribute status.
      Pitfalls & Limits:
    • Using nofollow instead of sponsored is acceptable but not fully aligned with latest Google guidelines.
    • Not tagging paid links risks manual penalties.
    • Overusing nofollow on natural outbound links can prevent trust signals.
      Example: New York Post correctly uses nofollow on Amazon affiliate links and paid promotion links, although they could switch to sponsored attributes for full compliance.
