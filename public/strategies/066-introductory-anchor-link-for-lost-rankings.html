Full Strategy Details

Introductory Anchor Link for Lost Rankings

What It Is

When a high-value keyword drops in rankings, create a new dedicated article targeting that keyword and add a single anchor link from the original top-ranking page's introductory paragraph pointing to this new page.

Why It Works

This tactic creates a relevant internal link that signals to Google renewed topical relevance and authority around the keyword, helping recover lost ranking positions quickly (typically within 3-4 days).

When To Use

Ideal for protecting and regaining rankings on high-traffic, high-conversion keywords that have slipped in position on your site. Useful for established sites managing important terms.

Execution Steps

  1. Identify the keyword for which your ranking has dropped.
  2. Write a new, focused article targeting this keyword.
  3. On the original page that lost ranking, insert one anchor link using the keyword phrase in the introductory paragraph pointing to the new article.
  4. Publish and monitor rankings over the next few days.

Pitfalls & Limits

  • Overuse or excessive internal linking in intros may dilute value.
  • The new article must be genuinely relevant and well-optimized.
  • Doesn’t replace the need for overall site authority and quality content.

Implementation Checklist

  1. Identify the keyword for which your ranking has dropped.
  2. Write a new, focused article targeting this keyword. On the original page that lost ranking, insert one anchor link using the keyword phrase in the introductory paragraph pointing to the new article.
  3. Publish and monitor rankings over the next few days.
