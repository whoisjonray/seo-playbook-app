Site Redesign with Proper 301 Redirects

Category: Technical SEO & Site Structure

Metadata

  • Type: Technical SEO & Site Structure
  • Difficulty: Intermediate
  • Time to Impact: 1-3 Months
  • Required Tools: None specified

Quick Summary

During website redesign or URL structure changes, properly implement 301 redirects from old URLs to new URLs to preserve SEO value and link equity.

Full Strategy Details

  1. Site Redesign with Proper 301 Redirects
  • What It Is: During website redesign or URL structure changes, properly implement 301 redirects from old URLs to new URLs to preserve SEO value and link equity.
    • Why It Works: 301 redirects signal to Google that content has permanently moved, transferring link juice and maintaining rankings. Without redirects, broken links occur, causing traffic and ranking losses.
    • When To Use: Any site redesign, migration, URL structure updates, or CMS changes.
    • Execution Steps:
      1. Inventory all old URLs before redesign, preferably via crawl or Google Analytics.
      2. Map old URLs to corresponding new URLs one-to-one.
      3. Implement 301 redirects at server or CMS level.
      4. Test redirects for accuracy and absence of redirect chains.
      5. Monitor rankings and traffic post-launch for issues.
    • Pitfalls & Limits: Failing to implement redirects causes loss of rankings; redirect chains or loops harm SEO; mismatched redirects confuse Google and users.
    • Example: A client plumber lost rankings after redesign because the previous agency failed to implement 301 redirects, which was reversed by applying correct redirects improving SEO recovery.
