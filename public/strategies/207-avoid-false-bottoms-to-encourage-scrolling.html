Avoid False Bottoms to Encourage Scrolling

Full Strategy Details

Avoid False Bottoms to Encourage Scrolling

  • What It Is: Design pages so visitors intuitively know there is more content below the visible screen to reduce bounce and increase engagement. Techniques include arrows pointing down or partial visibility of images.
    • Why It Works: Around 40% of users do scroll, but false bottoms create confusion, making users think the page ends prematurely, harming content consumption and conversions.
    • When To Use: All landing and product pages where multiple screenfuls of information exist and scrolling is needed to complete the buyer journey.
    • Execution Steps:
      1. Remove abrupt endings or large whitespace at the bottom of fold area.
      2. Add visual cues such as down arrows or partial images peeking into the fold area.
      3. Test different designs encouraging scrolling to see lift in engagement and conversions.
      4. Monitor bounce rate and page depth analytics to validate improvements.
    • Pitfalls & Limits: Overuse of arrows or clutter at bottom can be distracting. Some mobile designs may limit effectiveness.
    • Example: Adding a down arrow and exposing tops of images increased scroll rates and lowered exit rates.
