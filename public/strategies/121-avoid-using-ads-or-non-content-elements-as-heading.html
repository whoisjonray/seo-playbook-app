Full Strategy Details

Avoid Using Ads or Non-Content Elements as Heading Tags (H2, H3, etc.)

What It Is: Don’t mark advertisements or promotional content as heading tags; instead, use paragraph or div tags.
Why It Works: Heading tags signal content structure and hierarchy to search engines and assistive technologies. Misusing heading tags for ads can confuse crawlers and impair the page’s topical relevance.
When To Use: Sites with ads embedded in content or those wishing to maintain a clean semantic HTML structure.
Execution Steps:

  1. Audit pages for heading tags used for non-content elements such as ads.
  2. Replace heading tags surrounding ads with appropriate non-heading tags like <p> or <div>.
  3. Use CSS for styling instead of semantic HTML misuse.
    Pitfalls & Limits:
  • Leaving promotional content as headings dilutes keyword relevance and disrupts content flow.
  • Misuse may negatively impact accessibility and SEO clarity.
    Example: The New York Post mistakenly uses H2 tags for ad content, which is suboptimal.
