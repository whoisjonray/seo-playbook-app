Internal Linking to Target Keyword Pages

Internal Linking to Target Keyword Pages

Category: Link Building & Backlinks

Metadata

  • Type: Link Building & Backlinks
  • Difficulty: Intermediate
  • Time to Impact: 1-3 Months
  • Required Tools: None specified

Quick Summary

Ensuring internal links within a website point to pages targeting specific keywords to avoid orphan pages and boost page authority.

Full Strategy Details

  1. Internal Linking to Target Keyword Pages
    What It Is: Ensuring internal links within a website point to pages targeting specific keywords to avoid orphan pages and boost page authority.
    Why It Works: Google uses internal links to understand site structure, distribute link equity, and signal page importance. Orphan pages without internal links struggle to rank.
    When To Use: Small businesses, informational sites, and any site with content that targets specific keywords needing ranking support.
    Execution Steps:
    • Audit your site for pages without any internal links.
    • Identify target keywords per important page.
    • Add contextual internal links from other relevant pages (e.g., blog posts, service pages) to these pages using keyword-rich anchor text where natural.
    • Avoid using only menu links as the sole link source; include in-content links.
      Pitfalls & Limits: Overusing exact match anchors or linking from low-quality content can dilute value. Avoid random or forced linking.
      Example: Small business websites often lack internal links supporting keyword pages, hurting rankings.
