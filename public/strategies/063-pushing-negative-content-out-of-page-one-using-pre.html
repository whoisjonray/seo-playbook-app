Pushing Negative Content Out of Page One Using Press Releases

Pushing Negative Content Out of Page One Using Press Releases

Category: Reviews & Reputation

Metadata

  • Type: Reviews & Reputation
  • Difficulty: Intermediate
  • Time to Impact: 1-3 Months
  • Required Tools: Press Release Networks

Quick Summary

Using press releases to outrank negative or undesirable content on your branded or product-specific search terms by generating fresh, authoritative, and relevant content that Google prefers.

Full Strategy Details

  1. Pushing Negative Content Out of Page One Using Press Releases
    What It Is: Using press releases to outrank negative or undesirable content on your branded or product-specific search terms by generating fresh, authoritative, and relevant content that Google prefers.
    Why It Works: Google values freshness, trustworthiness, and authoritative sources, allowing newer, high-quality press releases to overtake old negative content in SERPs.
    When To Use: Particularly effective for reputation management when negative articles or reviews occupy page one of search results.
    Execution Steps:
    1. Identify negative or misleading posts ranking high on your brand queries.
    2. Draft a positive press release emphasizing your strengths, core messages, and improvements.
    3. Publish through an authoritative press release distribution network.
    4. Monitor SERP changes and adjust messages or issue follow-up releases as needed.
      Pitfalls & Limits:
    • Not a substitute for real reputation management; must also address core issues offline or via PR.
    • Negative posts with strong backlinks or authority may resist displacement.
      Example: Press release by fatgrid.com’s founder pushed a negative comparison article from #7 to #11 for their branded query, successfully moving it to page two.
