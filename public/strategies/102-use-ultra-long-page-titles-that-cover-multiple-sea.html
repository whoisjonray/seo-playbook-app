Full Strategy Details

Use Ultra-Long Page Titles That Cover Multiple Search Intents

What It Is

Write page titles significantly longer than the traditional 60-character limit, extending up to 150-250 characters, often combining 2-3 related title phrases separated by hyphens to capture diverse but relevant search intents.

Why It Works

Google often picks the most relevant segment of a longer title to match search intent, reducing the need for Google to rewrite title tags, and increasing rankings and click-through rates by better matching varied queries.

When To Use

Useful for pages targeting multiple related search intents or broad keyword variations on enterprise content sites, blogs, or service pages.

Execution Steps

  1. Identify 2-3 closely related keyword phrases or intents your page covers.
  2. Write a composite title tag that includes them, separated by hyphens or pipes.
  3. Ensure all parts of the title are relevant and natural to your content.
  4. Implement the new title tag in your HTML or CMS.
  5. Monitor search results to verify Google’s behavior and traffic changes.

Pitfalls & Limits

  • Titles that are too long or stuffed with keywords may appear spammy in SERPs or get truncated.
  • Not all queries may trigger Google to pick your segments as intended.

Implementation Checklist

  1. Identify 2 3 closely related keyword phrases or intents your page covers. Write a composite title tag that includes them, separated by hyphens or pipes.
  2. Ensure all parts of the title are relevant and natural to your content.
  3. Implement the new title tag in your HTML or CMS.
  4. Monitor search results to verify Google’s behavior and traffic changes.
