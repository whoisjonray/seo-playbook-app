Incentivizing Employees to Request Reviews (Avoid Incentivizing Customers)

  • Time to Impact: 1-3 Months
  • Required Tools: None specified

Quick Summary

Motivating employees via contests or rewards to actively solicit customer reviews without directly incentivizing customers.

Full Strategy Details

  1. Incentivizing Employees to Request Reviews (Avoid Incentivizing Customers)
    What It Is: Motivating employees via contests or rewards to actively solicit customer reviews without directly incentivizing customers.
    Why It Works: Employees who regularly ask satisfied customers increase review volume legitimately while avoiding Google guideline violations.
    When To Use: Local businesses wanting to boost Google reviews sustainably and within guidelines.
    Execution Steps:
    • Announce periodic contests or rewards for employees who generate the most reviews.
    • Train employees on how to request reviews tactfully and ethically.
    • Monitor review generation and publicly acknowledge employee efforts.
      Pitfalls & Limits: Never incentivize customers, as this can lead to penalties. Employee incentive programs need transparency and fairness.
      Example: Agency running review contests saw consistent review increases reliably.
