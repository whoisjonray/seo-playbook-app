Compact Keywords Focus

Full Strategy Details

Compact Keywords Focus

  • What It Is: A strategy that targets dozens of landing pages focused on high-purchase intent keywords, where each page is concise (average ~415 words) and designed to directly convert visitors rather than provide general information.
    • Why It Works: Targets buyers who are already ready to purchase, increasing conversion rates; shorter focused content is less work and ranks faster by filling keyword gaps, especially in less competitive languages or markets.
    • When To Use: Ecommerce, small business SEO, new or existing sites targeting transactional queries, multilingual or multi-regional SEO where ranking opportunities exist beyond English.
    • Execution Steps:
      1. Identify high purchase intent keywords with clear commercial value.
      2. Create dedicated landing pages (~400 words) optimized for those keywords.
      3. Avoid creating unnecessary content for informational queries unless strategically valuable.
      4. Ensure pages have clear calls to action designed to convert.
      5. Track rankings and conversions, focus on top 3 rankings for ecommerce due to SERP real estate dominated by ads and shopping.
    • Pitfalls & Limits: Ineffective if pages rank poorly (positions 5-10 result in minimal traffic), ineffective without good keyword research, and not suited for broad informational content where topical authority is needed.
    • Example: Client in men’s underwear niche in France ranked in top 3 for many keywords by focusing on purchase intent high-quality landing pages versus general content.

Implementation Checklist

  1. Identify high purchase intent keywords with clear commercial value.
  2. Create dedicated landing pages (~400 words) optimized for those keywords. Avoid creating unnecessary content for informational queries unless strategically valuable.
  3. Ensure pages have clear calls to action designed to convert.
  4. Track rankings and conversions, focus on top 3 rankings for ecommerce due to SERP real estate dominated by ads and shopping.
