Entity SEO Optimization

What It Is

Identify and incorporate relevant entities—keywords linked to Wikipedia pages and Google’s Knowledge Graph—into key on-page elements like the title, H1, H2, meta title, alt tags, and body content.

Why It Works

Google’s Knowledge Graph relies heavily on entities from authoritative sources like Wikipedia; including relevant entities helps Google better understand your content context and improves topical relevance signals, which can improve rankings.

When To Use

Content-rich pages targeting competitive or informational keywords, especially for niche or technical topics where entity recognition matters.

Execution Steps

  1. Identify your main keyword and relevant entities related to it (use tools like Schema Writer, Neuron Writer, Surfer SEO, Page Optimizer Pro, or manual Google Wikipedia searches).
  2. Verify relevant entities by searching “[keyword] Wikipedia” on Google and check which entities rank.
  3. Add the most relevant entity terms to all main ranking zones: title tag, meta title, H1 and H2 headings, body text, image alt texts.
  4. Ensure inclusion is natural and relevant so content remains user-friendly.
  5. Use ChatGPT or manual research to identify “entity gaps” by comparing your content’s entities with top-ranking competitors. Add missing relevant entities accordingly.

Pitfalls & Limits

Overuse or irrelevant entity inclusion can harm readability and user experience. Not all entities need to be used; prioritize relevance. This is not a silver bullet—entity signals alone won’t guarantee top rankings. Also, many tools only assist but do not automate the decision-making.
