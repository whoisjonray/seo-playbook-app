Evaluating Link Quality Using “Organic Pages” Metric Over Domain Authority

What It Is: Assessing potential linking sites by measuring how many of their pages rank well in Google (organic pages count) rather than just relying on generic domain authority metrics.
Why It Works: A site with many ranking pages indicates ongoing organic search health and link value; domain authority scores can be misleading if traffic or rankings have dropped.
When To Use: When vetting sites for guest posts, citations, or other link acquisitions.
Execution Steps:
- Use SEO tools (e.g., Ahrefs) to analyze a site’s organic pages metric.
- Prefer links from sites with a consistently high number of ranking pages.
- Avoid links from ticking domain authority “mountains” with little to no organic traffic or ranking pages.
Pitfalls & Limits: This requires access to advanced SEO tools; newer sites with low organic footprint can’t be measured this way.
Example: Many guest posting sites showed high domain ratings but very few ranking pages and tanking traffic, indicating links from these sites were likely worthless.

Implementation Checklist

  • Use SEO tools (e.g., Ahrefs) to analyze a site’s organic pages metric.
  • Prefer links from sites with a consistently high number of ranking pages.
  • Avoid links from ticking domain authority “mountains” with little to no organic traffic or ranking pages.
