Including Keywords in URL Slugs for Ranking Benefit

Category: Reviews & Reputation

Metadata

  • Type: Reviews & Reputation
  • Difficulty: Beginner
  • Time to Impact: 1-3 Months
  • Required Tools: None specified

Quick Summary

Using descriptive, keyword-rich words in page URL slugs rather than overly short or generic ones.

Full Strategy Details

  1. Including Keywords in URL Slugs for Ranking Benefit
    What It Is: Using descriptive, keyword-rich words in page URL slugs rather than overly short or generic ones.
    Why It Works: Keywords in URLs give Google and users explicit context about page content and improve relevance signals.
    When To Use: All website pages, especially service and product pages targeting specific keywords.
    Execution Steps:
    • Review existing URL slugs on your site.
    • Add relevant keywords naturally into the slug without keyword stuffing.
    • Avoid overly short URLs that omit target keywords.
    • Maintain simple URL structure without unnecessary folders if possible.
      Pitfalls & Limits: Avoid creating extremely long URLs or using poor keyword stuffing; changing URLs on live sites requires proper redirects.
      Example: URLs with relevant keywords like /services/plumber-chicago rank better than generic slugs like /services/page1.

Implementation Checklist

  • Review existing URL slugs on your site.
  • Add relevant keywords naturally into the slug without keyword stuffing.
  • Avoid overly short URLs that omit target keywords.
  • Maintain simple URL structure without unnecessary folders if possible.
