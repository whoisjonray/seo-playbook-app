Quick Summary

Identify and repair or update broken internal links within your site.

Full Strategy Details

  1. Fixing Broken Internal Links to Enhance Crawlability and User Experience
  • What It Is: Identify and repair or update broken internal links within your site.
    • Why It Works: Broken links disrupt crawl flow and user navigation, harming SEO by wasting crawl budget and creating poor user experience signals.
    • When To Use: All sites as ongoing maintenance, particularly after site migrations, redesigns, or content updates.
    • Execution Steps:
      1. Use crawling tools (e.g., Screaming Frog, Ahrefs Site Audit) to find broken internal links.
      2. Update or remove broken links pointing to deleted or moved pages.
      3. Replace links with the correct URLs or relink to relevant content.
    • Pitfalls & Limits: Over-fixing minor internal links without focus wastes time; duplicated content pages can confuse internal linking; some broken links may be unavoidable temporarily (redirects help here).
