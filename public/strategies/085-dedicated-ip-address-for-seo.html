Quick Summary

Using a dedicated IP address for your website hosting instead of a shared IP address that hosts multiple websites.

Full Strategy Details

  1. Dedicated IP Address for SEO
    What It Is: Using a dedicated IP address for your website hosting instead of a shared IP address that hosts multiple websites.
    Why It Works: A dedicated IP can prevent the "bad neighborhood" effect, where other sites sharing the same IP might be spammy or blacklisted, potentially impacting your SEO rank by association. The unique dedicated IP avoids this risk.
    When To Use: Suitable for sites concerned with potential negative SEO impact from shared IP bad neighbors, such as ecommerce, enterprise sites, and authority websites that rely heavily on SEO rankings.
    Execution Steps:
    1. Contact your web host to upgrade your hosting plan to include a dedicated IP.
    2. Update your DNS settings to point your domain to the new dedicated IP.
    3. Verify that the site resolves correctly and monitor search rankings post-migration.
      Pitfalls & Limits: Dedicated IPs do not guarantee better rankings alone; if the shared IP is clean, the impact is minimal. Upgrading can incur additional costs. Also, a bad site on your dedicated IP (if shared later) could still hurt you. This tactic is less important if you use a reputable CDN or well-managed hosting.
      Example: In the SEO experiments referenced, a site on a dedicated IP ranked 9 out of 10 times higher compared to the same site on a shared IP, which ranked only once at position 8.
