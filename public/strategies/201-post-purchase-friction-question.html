Post-Purchase Friction Question

Post-Purchase Friction Question

Category: Advanced Tactics

Metadata

  • Type: Advanced Tactics
  • Difficulty: Intermediate
  • Time to Impact: Hours to Days
  • Required Tools: None specified

Quick Summary

Add a question on your thank you page (immediately after purchase) asking customers, “What was the one thing that almost made you not buy?” to identify friction points.

Full Strategy Details

  1. Post-Purchase Friction Question
  • What It Is: Add a question on your thank you page (immediately after purchase) asking customers, “What was the one thing that almost made you not buy?” to identify friction points.
    • Why It Works: Collects immediate, relevant, fresh feedback from buyers who just experienced your site, revealing barriers or uncertainties that may have almost stopped sales. This direct insight helps improve UX/UI and conversion.
    • When To Use: Ecommerce sites, any business with an online purchase funnel, especially if you want quick qualitative feedback on purchase obstacles.
    • Execution Steps:
      1. Implement a popup or embedded form on the order confirmation/thank you page asking the friction question.
      2. Use tools like Lucky Orange or other form providers to collect responses.
      3. Monitor responses regularly, ideally aiming for 30%+ response rate.
      4. Analyze data to identify frequent issues or misunderstandings.
      5. Update site copy, UX elements, or product information to remove or reduce those friction points.
    • Pitfalls & Limits: Not applicable for browsers who abandoned without purchase; only reflects buyers who completed checkout. May be ignored if poorly phrased or inconvenient to answer.
    • Example: Used on multiple sites, responsible for 6 out of 10 biggest conversion wins worth multiple millions in revenue over several years.

Implementation Checklist
