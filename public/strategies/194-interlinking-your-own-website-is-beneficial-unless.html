Full Strategy Details

Interlinking Your Own Website is Beneficial Unless Used Manipulatively

What It Is: Proper internal linking within your own site improves site structure, distributes link equity, and helps Google crawl, contrary to the myth that it hurts SEO.
Why It Works: Google uses internal links to understand site hierarchy, topical relevance, and to find new content quickly, improving overall SEO performance.
When To Use: All websites, especially content-heavy sites, blogs, ecommerce for category-product linking.
Execution Steps:

  • Audit existing internal links.
  • Add relevant links between related pages using descriptive anchor text.
  • Avoid excessive or spammy linking for manipulative intent (e.g., PBNs).
  • Update internal linking as content grows or changes.
    Pitfalls & Limits: Trying to manipulate rankings through excessive cross-site linking or PBN (private blog network) style hosting setups can backfire.
    Example: Misconception debunked that interlinking or hosting multiple sites on same hosting is penalized; only manipulative PBN behavior is risky.
