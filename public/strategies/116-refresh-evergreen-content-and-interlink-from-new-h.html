Refresh Evergreen Content and Interlink from New High-Quality Content

Category: Link Building & Backlinks

Metadata

  • Type: Link Building & Backlinks
  • Difficulty: Intermediate
  • Time to Impact: 1-3 Months
  • Required Tools: None specified

Quick Summary

Regularly update content that should rank consistently (evergreen) and link to it from newly published, well-optimized content to maintain and boost rankings.

Full Strategy Details

  1. Refresh Evergreen Content and Interlink from New High-Quality Content
    What It Is: Regularly update content that should rank consistently (evergreen) and link to it from newly published, well-optimized content to maintain and boost rankings.
    Why It Works: Updating content signals freshness and relevance to Google; interlinking from new content passes authority and improves crawl frequency.
    When To Use: Blogs, resource hubs, informational sites with content meant to rank long-term.
    Execution Steps:
  2. Identify evergreen content that may be losing traffic or ranking.
  3. Update the content with fresh information, current data, or improved UX.
  4. Publish new content that is topically related and optimized for current keywords.
  5. Within the new content, add internal links pointing to refreshed evergreen pages.
  6. Resubmit updated pages in Google Search Console to prompt re-indexing.
    Pitfalls & Limits: Frequent unnecessary updates or low-quality new content can dilute value. Interlinking must be natural and relevant, not spammy.
    Example: Practitioners have seen faster ranking improvements by combining refreshes and strategic internal linking between old and new content.
